Letters of Recommendation (LOR) Guide

Letters of Recommendation (LORs) are arguably the most heavily weighted component of a PhD application. While grades and test scores represent standard criteria, LORs represent professional testimonies about your potential to succeed as an independent scholar.

1. The Recommender Composition

Almost all US programs require three letters of recommendation. For a research degree, the ideal combination is:

  • **Letter 1: Primary Research Advisor.** This person oversaw your thesis, independent studies, or research assistantships, and can write in detail about your research methodology, coding, or mathematical proofs.
  • **Letter 2: Secondary Research Mentor.** A professor from a summer research program (REU), co-advisor, or research collaborator.
  • **Letter 3: Advanced Course Instructor.** A professor who taught you in an upper-level, proof-based class (e.g., Topology, Functional Analysis) where you excelled and participated actively.

2. Asking for Letters: The Etiquette

Never request a letter via a short email the week before submissions. Instead, structure your request professionally:

Draft Email Template:
- **Start early:** Contact them in September.
- **Schedule a short meeting:** Ask to talk for 15 minutes about your graduate school goals.
- **Explicit Question:** Ask, *"Do you feel comfortable writing me a **strong** letter of recommendation for Applied Math PhD programs?"*

"If a professor hesitates or says they can only write a general letter, thank them and find another recommender. A weak or generic letter ('Hoang got an A in my class') is a red flag to admissions committees."

3. Providing a "Recommender Info Packet"

To help your writers craft highly detailed letters, email them an info packet containing:

  1. Your current CV/Resume.
  2. Your academic transcript (unofficial is fine).
  3. Your Statement of Purpose draft.
  4. A table listing your target schools, application deadlines, program names, and why you are applying to each (e.g., specific advisor names).
