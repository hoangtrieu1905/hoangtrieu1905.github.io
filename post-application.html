Interviews, Offers & Deciding

Once your applications are submitted in December, the waiting period begins. By mid-January and February, universities start sending out interview invitations, open house requests, and funding offers. This final chapter covers how to navigate this final stretch.

1. The PhD Interview: What to Expect

Unlike corporate interviews, a PhD interview is an academic discussion. Professors want to evaluate how you think about research and whether you can hold a dialogue about your field of interest.

  • **Know your CV:** Be prepared to explain any research project, theorem, or library mentioned in your resume.
  • **Read their recent work:** Look up the interviewer's recent papers. You don't need to understand every detail, but you should understand the general problem they are solving.
  • **Prepare questions:** Ask about their mentoring style, typical graduation timelines for their students, and funding availability.

2. Managing Multiple Offers

Receiving multiple admissions offers is an excellent problem to have, but it requires careful handling. In the US, the Council of Graduate Schools has a national agreement where you have until **April 15th** to make your final decision. You should never feel pressured by a department to accept an offer before this deadline.

"Use your leverage to visit campuses. The department will pay for your flights and lodging to attend their open houses. This is your best chance to talk to current students face-to-face and inspect lab conditions."

3. The Final Decision: Key Metrics

When choosing between programs, look past university rankings and evaluate these three indicators:

  1. **Advisor Mentorship:** Will you get along with your primary advisor for the next 5 years? Do their current students graduate on time?
  2. **Financial Security:** Does the stipend cover the local cost of living comfortably? How many semesters of TA work are required before you can transition to RA?
  3. **Location & Community:** You will be living there for half a decade. Choose a city and a cohort where you feel welcome and supported.

Deciding checklist: Once you make your choice, notify your chosen program, decline other offers promptly so waitlisted students can receive funding, and write thank-you notes to your recommenders who helped you along the way.
