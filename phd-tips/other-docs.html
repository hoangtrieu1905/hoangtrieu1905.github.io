Academic Resume & Other Documents

By reading this section, you will be able to answer the following questions:

  • How should I tailor my academic resume?
  • What documents do international students need to submit?
  • What is an Explanation of Curriculums, and why should I consider submitting one?

Academic Resume

I don’t have too many comments on this section. My resume was prepared in Latex and tailored by AI. That was encouraged by Augustana College’s career center. My only recommendation is to focus on your contributions rather than re-write the whole job description.

(International Students) Other Documents

  1. Visa
    Since I’m a current F1-visa student in the US, I only need to submit a picture of my current US visa and the photo page of my passport.
  2. Verification of Enrollment and English Proficiency
    Some programs I applied to require this document as proof of enrollment and English proficiency. I did not submit any of the IELTS, Toelf, etc. tests because I have a letter from my school’s Registrar Office confirming that my curriculum at Augustana College was entirely taught in English. If you are applying as an international student going to a college/ university abroad, you will need other methods to prove your English Proficiency.

Explanation of Curriculums

The previous sections are technically the required parts of a PhD application. However, I also submitted a Explanation of Curriculums. The reason is simple: I’m not confident in my research, and thus I tried to use coursework as a supplement to my ability to be successful in graduate school.

In short, this is a detailed excel table with detailed of:

  • Advanced courses I took
  • The letter grades or the expected grades
  • The textbook I used
  • The topics that were covered in the course

If you are a transfer student, it helps to have a written explanation of all the institutions you attended. Overcommunicating is better than confusion of information.
