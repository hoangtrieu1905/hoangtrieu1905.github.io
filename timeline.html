PhD Application Timeline

Applying to graduate school is an exercise in project management. Since deadlines for most US PhD programs fall between December 1st and January 15th, you should begin preparing your application materials at least 6 to 9 months in advance.

1. Phase 1: Summer Planning (June – August)

The summer before your senior year is all about exploration and organization. Do not rush into writing drafts; instead focus on research.

  • **June:** Identify your specific research fields and compile a spreadsheet detailing potential schools, advisor names, application deadlines, and fee waiver opportunities.
  • **July:** Begin reading papers written by professors on your list. Make detailed notes of open questions or methods you find intriguing.
  • **August:** Draft your academic CV/Resume. If required, study and register for the GRE/Subject GRE (though many math programs have made the GRE optional).

2. Phase 2: Material Assembly (September – October)

This is the most labor-intensive phase, where your statements and recommendations begin to form.

  • **September:** Reach out to potential faculty advisors via email. Contact your undergraduate professors to request Letters of Recommendation.
  • **October:** Draft your first Statement of Purpose (SOP). Focus on your research experience (e.g., Maxwell solvers, thesis topics) and future research questions.

"Always give your recommenders at least 6 to 8 weeks before the deadline. Send them your CV, transcript, and Statement of Purpose draft so they can write highly specific letters."

3. Phase 3: Final Polish and Submission (November – December)

Submitting applications early or precisely on time ensures everything is in order before the holiday shutdown.

  • **November:** Solicit feedback on your SOP from advisors, colleagues, and writing tutors. Polish your diversity and personal statements. Request official transcripts.
  • **December:** Fill out portal questionnaires. Submit your applications. Keep track of recommender submission status in your spreadsheet.

Pro-Tip: Check fee waiver deadlines! Many departments offer fee waivers for students who apply before a specific early deadline (often in November), or who attend virtual information sessions.

4. Phase 4: Interviews & Offers (January – April)

The cycle completes as you receive invitation requests, attend open houses, and weigh funding offers before the national deadline on **April 15th**.
