Academic CV, Transcripts & Fee Waivers

While the Statement of Purpose and Letters of Recommendation are the core pillars of your application, neglecting supporting materials can weaken your package. In this chapter, we outline strategies for transcript collection, academic CV formatting, and securing application fee waivers.

1. The Academic CV (Curriculum Vitae)

Unlike a professional resume which fits on a single page, an academic CV can span multiple pages. In STEM fields, structure your CV to prioritize research output:

  • **Education:** List your degree, GPA (and major GPA), honors, and relevant coursework.
  • **Research Experience:** Detail your thesis, assistantships, and independent study topics. Explain your contributions clearly.
  • **Publications & Preprints:** List any completed manuscripts, thesis titles, or conference presentations.
  • **Skills:** Categorize by programming languages (Python, Java), mathematical packages (MATLAB, R), and libraries (NumPy, PyTorch).

2. Academic Transcripts

Many programs allow you to upload **unofficial transcripts** during the initial submission phase. However, ensure that your scanned copies are legible. Check if your university records major GPAs separately; highlighting major GPA in your CV or application portal is useful if your general GPA was dragged down by introductory courses.

3. Fee Waivers: Don't Pay Application Fees

Applying to 8–10 graduate programs can quickly accumulate $800 to $1,000 in application fees. However, many departments offer fee waivers. Common waiver categories include:

Fee Waiver Options:
- **Societies & Affiliations:** Being a member of Pi Mu Epsilon, McNair Scholars, or other recognized honor societies.
- **Information Sessions:** Many departments waive fees for students who register and attend their graduate program virtual webinars in October/November.
- **Financial Need:** Contacting the graduate admissions coordinator directly to explain financial constraints.

"Request fee waivers early. Departmental budgets for fee waivers are finite and are often granted on a first-come, first-served basis during October."
