Places That Have
Shaped Me

Every place leaves something behind — a different way of thinking about people, food, mathematics, or just what a good afternoon looks like.

🇻🇳

Vietnam

Born and Raised

Where everything started. The food, the people, the pace of life — everything I compare new places to. Coming back always resets something in me that I didn't know had drifted.

🇺🇸

United States

Home Away from Home

Augustana College in Rock Island, IL — the town where I grew as a mathematician, teacher, and person. Illinois winters included. The Mississippi River is bigger than it looks on a map.

🇭🇺

Hungary

Budapest Semester · Spring 2025

The Budapest Semester in Mathematics changed how I think about math. The city itself is stunning — the Parliament by the Danube at night, the ruin bars in the 7th district. But it's the problems and the people you solve them with that stay with you.

🇦🇹

Austria

Day Trip from Budapest

Vienna in a day — Schönbrunn Palace, too much coffee, and the realization that the Viennese take dessert very seriously. The Kunsthistorisches Museum was worth every minute.

🇸🇰

Slovakia

Weekend Trip

Bratislava is one of those cities that feels overlooked — which means you have it mostly to yourself. The old town at dusk is genuinely lovely. Cross the border from Budapest and you're there in two hours.

🇩🇪

Germany

Weekend Trip

The country where I started learning German. Berlin's energy is unlike anywhere else — messy, creative, constantly rebuilding itself. The East Side Gallery is a reminder of how recently everything changed.

🇧🇪

Belgium

Weekend Trip

Brussels and Bruges. Belgian waffles are not a myth. The medieval canal system in Bruges felt like stepping into a painting. The art museums were an unexpected highlight.

🇳🇱

The Netherlands

Weekend Trip

Amsterdam is everything you've heard, and the canal system is genuinely impressive once you realize it was all engineered. Time it right and the tulip fields are in bloom. Rijksmuseum is worth the full day.

🇸🇮

Slovenia

Weekend Trip

Lake Bled is not overrated. Ljubljana is a small capital that feels like it has figured something out that bigger cities haven't — walkable, green, genuinely pleasant. My favorite discovery from the Budapest semester trips.

🇮🇹

Italy

Weekend Trip

Rome, Florence, or Venice — each deserves more time than a weekend. The food alone is reason enough to come back. Standing in the Pantheon, a 2000-year-old unreinforced concrete dome, is genuinely humbling.

🇬🇷

Greece

Weekend Trip

Athens and the Acropolis. Standing in the same city where mathematics was formalized thousands of years ago makes you feel both very small and very lucky. The souvlaki was also excellent.
